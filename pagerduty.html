PagerDuty Ads

PROBLEM: PagerDuty was growing through word-of-mouth and minimal advertising, but needed a design-led ad campaign to drive free trial registrations at scale.

SOLUTION: Brought in a copywriter, ran stakeholder research to uncover the right message—time and stress relief—and built a campaign that spoke directly to IT practitioners' real pain points.

ROLE: Art Director

How We Did It

Made the case for a copywriter

PagerDuty had never worked with a copywriter. Pitched the idea to stakeholders and got buy-in, then brought in the right creative partner to develop researched, targeted headlines. Not just executing design—proving the value of the entire creative process.

Found the insight that mattered

Listened to marketers and educators to understand what PagerDuty actually fixed. Narrowed hundreds of concepts down to two core ideas: time and stress relief—pain points every IT professional deeply felt.

Built a visual system from the ground up

With no formal brand guidelines, developed an abstracted low-poly visual system built on existing brand equity. Revived a beloved piece of company lore—"Relax, We're On PagerDuty"—as a retro-toned campaign anchor that resonated immediately.

Impact

Created PagerDuty's first design-led ad campaign framework

Drove free trial registrations through targeted, insight-backed creative

Established visual standards and a copy process where neither had existed before
